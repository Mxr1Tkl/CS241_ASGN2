One of the most widely publicized cases is that of his nephew, Waisake Naholo, a Fijian-born rugby star playing for the All Blacks. Claims & Controversy

Naiova describes his gift as spiritual and hereditary; he does not charge for treatments, believing his ability is a blessing from God. He also claims he sometimes carries kawakawarau leaves to rugby matches so he can provide aid if injuries occur during the game.

However, his claims attract skepticism. Western medical practitioners emphasize the lack of controlled scientific studies confirming that such leaf-based treatments can repair bone fractures. Some observers suggest that spontaneous healing, placebo effects, or anecdotal exaggeration may explain these dramatic stories.
